Cryptocurrency

Cryptocurrency is basically digital money that doesn’t rely on banks. A fun fact is that the first big one, Bitcoin, started in 2009. It became popular because people liked the idea of having money that wasn’t controlled by governments. It shows that society can collectively agree to give value to something we can’t even touch or see, just because we trust the system behind it.

Key Vocabulary

  • Computing Device: A physical device that can run a program (like a computer, smartphone, or sensor).
  • Computer Network: A group of devices that can send or receive data between each other.
  • Protocol: The rules that computers agree on so they can communicate.
  • Fault Tolerant: When a system still works even if some parts fail.
