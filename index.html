Cryptocurrency

Cryptocurrency is like digital money that only exists online. It’s not physical or anything you can hold, but people still use it to buy stuff or invest. One fun fact is that the first real purchase using Bitcoin was two pizzas. Crypto became popular because it was new, exciting, and people liked the idea of money that wasn’t controlled by banks or governments. It shows how humans can give value to something you can’t even see—like agreeing together that numbers on a screen are worth thousands of dollars.

The Internet

The internet is what connects billions of computers and phones so they can talk to each other. It’s like a giant invisible web that lets us send memes, stream videos, and even do homework.

Vocab Words

  • Computing Device: A physical device that can run a program (like a computer or phone).
  • Computer Network: A group of devices connected together that can send/receive data.
  • Protocol: Rules that tell systems how to behave and communicate.
  • Bandwidth: The maximum capacity of data a system can send (bits per second).
